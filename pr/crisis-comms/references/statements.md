## The message house (build this first)

Before drafting any single statement, build the **message house** — one page that every external and internal message draws from. It guarantees consistency across spokespeople, channels, and days.

```
ROOF (the umbrella message — one sentence):
  The single thing you want every audience to take away.

PILLARS (2–4 supporting messages):
  Pillar 1 — Acknowledgment / empathy
  Pillar 2 — Action being taken
  Pillar 3 — Accountability / what changes
  (Pillar 4 — Reassurance / what stakeholders should do)

FOUNDATION (proof points):
  Verified facts, figures, and actions that support each pillar.
  Everything here must trace to the fact log.
```

Tailor framing per audience, but never let the pillars contradict each other across audiences.

## Holding statement

Issued fast, before facts are complete. Goal: occupy the space, demonstrate awareness and care, buy verification time.

**Anatomy (≈3–5 sentences):**
1. Acknowledgment — name the situation plainly.
2. Empathy — concern for those affected (people first).
3. Action — what you are doing right now.
4. Commitment — when you'll say more.
5. (Optional) Direction — what affected people should do now / where to get updates.

**Template — operational incident (outage / breach, early):**
> We are aware of [an issue affecting / a security incident involving] [system or service], and we understand how concerning this is for [affected group]. Our team is working urgently to [investigate / contain / restore], and protecting [your data / your safety / your service] is our priority. We will provide a verified update by [specific time]. For the latest information, please visit [owned channel].

**Template — emerging reputational story:**
> We take [the concerns raised / these allegations] seriously and are looking into the matter carefully. We are committed to [relevant value], and we will share what we learn once we have established the facts. We expect to provide further information by [time].

## Full statement

Issued once facts are established. Longer, but still tight. Structure:

1. **Acknowledgment + empathy** — restate the situation and human impact.
2. **What happened** — verified facts only: what, when, scope. No speculation on cause if unconfirmed.
3. **Responsibility** — calibrated to the SCCT cluster (see `crisis-typology.md`): inform (victim), explain (accidental), or apologize and own (preventable).
4. **Action** — concrete steps taken and underway, with specifics (not "we take this seriously").
5. **Remediation for those affected** — what you're offering / what they should do.
6. **Prevention** — what changes so it doesn't recur.
7. **Contact / source** — where to get verified updates.

**Template — preventable crisis (high responsibility):**
> On [date] we [plainly state what happened]. This should not have happened, and we are sorry. [Affected group] trusted us, and we let them down.
> Here is what we know: [verified scope — who/what affected, how many, over what period].
> We are taking full responsibility. We have already [action 1], [action 2], and [action 3]. For those affected, we are [remediation — refund / credit monitoring / replacement / direct contact].
> To prevent this from happening again, we are [structural change]. We will report on our progress by [date].
> If you have questions, [contact / channel].

## Media Q&A / reactive lines

Prepare answers to the hardest questions before the spokesperson faces them. For each, write a **bridge** (acknowledge the question, then move to a message-house pillar) without dodging.

- Anticipate: "How did this happen?" / "How many affected?" / "Who's responsible?" / "Why didn't you prevent it?" / "Will heads roll?" / "Is it safe now?"
- For facts not yet confirmed: "I don't have a verified number yet; I won't speculate — what I can tell you is [confirmed fact + action]. We'll share the figure the moment it's confirmed."
- Never repeat a hostile premise (the negative phrasing gets quoted). Reframe to your own words.

## Line-by-line do / don't

| Do | Don't |
| --- | --- |
| "We were hacked and some customer data was taken." | "A sophisticated threat actor accessed certain environments." |
| "We're sorry. This was our mistake." | "Mistakes were made." (agentless = evasive) |
| Give a specific next-update time. | "We'll update in due course." |
| Lead with the people affected. | Lead with revenue, share price, or inconvenience to the company. |
| Confirmed numbers only, or none. | Round guesses that you'll have to revise downward in public. |
| "Here's what to do to protect yourself: …" | Leave affected people with no action. |

## Common failure patterns to avoid
- **The non-denial denial** that technically lies — fatal once contradicted.
- **Over-lawyering** until the statement says nothing human.
- **Premature precision** — committing to a cause or count before verification, then walking it back.
- **The disappearing act** — going silent and letting others narrate.
- **Tone mismatch** — corporate cheer over real human harm, or melodrama over a minor issue.
